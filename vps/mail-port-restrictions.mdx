---
title: Mail port restrictions
description: Why outbound mail ports are restricted on SpeedyPage VPS servers and how to request access.
---

SpeedyPage restricts outbound mail ports on all VPS servers to prevent spam and protect the reputation of our IP address ranges. The following ports are blocked by default:

| Port | Protocol |
| --- | --- |
| 25 | SMTP — standard email sending |
| 465 | SMTPS — SMTP over SSL/TLS |
| 587 | Submission — email submission (RFC 6409) |
| 2525 | Alternative SMTP |

## Why these ports are restricted

- **Spam prevention** — blocks unauthorised bulk email from newly provisioned servers
- **IP reputation** — protects deliverability for all customers on the network
- **Security** — reduces the risk of compromised servers being used for malware distribution

## Request access

If you have a legitimate need to send email from your VPS:

1. Log in to your [client area](https://my.speedypage.com) and go to your VPS service
2. Scroll to the **SMTP Port Access** section
3. Describe your use case and the reason you need access
4. Submit the request and wait for review

Requests are reviewed individually. You'll receive a response with either approval or further questions about your setup.
