---
title: Manage DNS records
description: How to add and edit DNS records using the Zone Editor in cPanel.
---

If your domain uses SpeedyPage nameservers (`ns1.speedy.page` and `ns2.speedy.page`), you manage DNS records through cPanel's Zone Editor.

<Note>
If your domain uses an external DNS provider (e.g., Cloudflare, your registrar's DNS), manage DNS records at that provider instead of in cPanel.
</Note>

## DNS record types

| Record type | Purpose | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| A | Points a domain or subdomain to an IPv4 address | `example.com` → `203.0.113.10` |
| AAAA | Points a domain or subdomain to an IPv6 address | `example.com` → `2001:db8::1` |
| CNAME | Creates an alias pointing to another domain | `www.example.com` → `example.com` |
| MX | Routes email to a mail server | `example.com` → `mail.example.com` (priority 10) |
| TXT | Stores text data for verification, SPF, DKIM, and other purposes | `v=spf1 +a +mx ~all` |

## Access the Zone Editor

1. Log in to cPanel through your [client area](https://my.speedypage.com) or by visiting `yourdomain.com/cpanel`
2. In the **Domains** section, click **Zone Editor**
3. Find your domain in the list and click **Manage**

## Add a DNS record

This example walks through adding an A record. The process is similar for other record types.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Zone Editor">
    In cPanel, go to **Domains** → **Zone Editor** and click **Manage** next to your domain.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a record">
    Click **Add Record**. A new row appears at the top of the record list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the record">
    Fill in the following fields:

    - **Name** — the hostname (e.g., `example.com` for the root domain, or `sub.example.com` for a subdomain)
    - **TTL** — time to live in seconds (default `14400` is fine for most cases)
    - **Type** — select **A** from the dropdown
    - **Record** — the IPv4 address to point to (e.g., `203.0.113.10`)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the record">
    Click **Save Record**. The DNS change propagates within the TTL period.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Edit or delete a record

From the Zone Editor:

- Click **Edit** next to a record to modify its value, TTL, or type
- Click **Delete** to remove a record

<Warning>
Be careful when editing or deleting MX records. Incorrect MX records will prevent your domain from receiving email.
</Warning>

## Common DNS configurations

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Point your domain to a SpeedyPage hosting account">
    Create an A record for your root domain (`example.com`) pointing to your hosting account's IP address. You can find your IP address in cPanel under **Server Information** or on the right-hand sidebar of the cPanel home page.

    Also create a CNAME record for `www.example.com` pointing to `example.com` so both versions work.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Set up email routing with MX records">
    MX records tell other mail servers where to deliver email for your domain. If you're using SpeedyPage's email, the MX record is configured automatically.

    To use an external email provider (e.g., Google Workspace, Microsoft 365), delete the existing MX records and add the records provided by your email provider.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Add a TXT record for domain verification">
    Services like Google Search Console, Microsoft 365, and others often require a TXT record to verify domain ownership. Add a TXT record with the **Name** set to your domain and the **Record** set to the verification string provided by the service.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Set up SPF for email authentication">
    An SPF record tells receiving mail servers which IP addresses are authorized to send email for your domain. For SpeedyPage hosting, the recommended SPF record is:

    ```text
    v=spf1 +a +mx include:_spf.mailchannels.net ~all
    ```

    Add this as a TXT record with the **Name** set to your domain.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>
DNS changes can take up to 24-48 hours to fully propagate worldwide, though most changes are visible within a few hours. The actual time depends on the TTL of the previous record and caching by DNS resolvers.
</Tip>
