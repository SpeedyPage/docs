---
title: Configure spam filtering
description: How to enable and configure SpamAssassin spam filtering in cPanel on your SpeedyPage hosting plan.
---

SpeedyPage includes SpamAssassin on all hosting plans. SpamAssassin scores incoming emails based on spam characteristics and filters messages that exceed a set threshold.

## Enable SpamAssassin

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to cPanel">
    Access cPanel through your [client area](https://my.speedypage.com) or by visiting `yourdomain.com/cpanel`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Spam Filters">
    In the **Email** section, click **Spam Filters**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable SpamAssassin">
    Toggle **Process New Emails and Mark them as Spam** to on.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Adjust the spam score threshold

Every incoming email receives a spam score. The higher the score, the more likely the message is spam. The default threshold is **5** — any message scoring 5 or above is marked as spam.

To change the threshold:

1. On the **Spam Filters** page, click **Spam Threshold Score**
2. Select a new score value
3. Click **Update Scoring Options**

| Score | Filtering level |
| --- | --- |
| 8-10 | Lenient — only the most obvious spam is caught |
| 5 (default) | Balanced — catches most spam with few false positives |
| 1-4 | Aggressive — catches more spam but may flag legitimate email |

<Warning>
Setting the score too low (1-2) can cause legitimate emails to be marked as spam. Start with the default of 5 and adjust gradually if needed.
</Warning>

## Auto-delete spam

You can configure SpamAssassin to automatically delete messages that exceed the spam score threshold instead of delivering them to your spam folder:

1. On the **Spam Filters** page, toggle **Automatically Delete New Spam (Auto-Delete)** to on
2. Confirm the action when prompted

<Warning>
Auto-deleted messages cannot be recovered. Only enable this after you've confirmed the spam threshold is not flagging legitimate email.
</Warning>

## Spam box filtering

When SpamAssassin is enabled without auto-delete, spam messages are delivered to a **spam** (or **junk**) folder in your mailbox. Check this folder periodically for false positives — legitimate messages that were incorrectly flagged.

<Tip>
If a specific sender's email is consistently marked as spam, you can add their address to the SpamAssassin whitelist in cPanel to ensure their messages are always delivered to your inbox.
</Tip>
