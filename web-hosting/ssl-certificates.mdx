All SpeedyPage Web Hosting plans include free SSL certificates through AutoSSL, powered by Sectigo. AutoSSL automatically provisions and renews certificates for every domain and subdomain on your account.

## Check your SSL status

1. Log in to cPanel through your [client area](https://my.speedypage.com) or by visiting `yourdomain.com/cpanel`
2. Go to the **Security** section and click **SSL/TLS Status**
3. Review the list of domains — a green padlock indicates an active certificate

## Run AutoSSL manually

AutoSSL runs automatically, but you can trigger it on demand if a certificate hasn't been provisioned yet.

1. In cPanel, go to **SSL/TLS Status**
2. Click **Run AutoSSL**
3. Wait for the process to complete — this usually takes a few minutes

<Note>
AutoSSL checks run periodically and will automatically provision certificates for new domains. Manual runs are only needed if you want to speed up the process.
</Note>

## Troubleshoot AutoSSL failures

If AutoSSL fails to issue a certificate, check the following:

- **DNS is pointing to SpeedyPage** — your domain must resolve to your hosting account. Ensure your nameservers are set to `ns1.speedy.page` and `ns2.speedy.page`. See [nameserver setup](/web-hosting/nameservers) for details.
- **No conflicting DNS records** — CAA records can block certificate issuance. If you have CAA records, add one for `sectigo.com`.
- **Domain is reachable** — the domain must respond on port 80 for validation to succeed. Ensure no firewall rules or redirects are blocking access.

<Warning>
AutoSSL cannot issue certificates for domains that don't resolve to your SpeedyPage hosting account. If you're using external DNS, create the correct A record pointing to your server's IP address.
</Warning>

## Install a custom SSL certificate

If you have a certificate from another provider, you can install it manually.

1. In cPanel, go to the **Security** section and click **SSL/TLS**
2. Click **Install and Manage SSL for your sites (HTTPS)**
3. Select the domain from the dropdown
4. Paste your certificate (CRT), private key, and certificate authority bundle (CABUNDLE) into the corresponding fields
5. Click **Install Certificate**

<Tip>
Custom certificates take priority over AutoSSL. If you later remove a custom certificate, AutoSSL will automatically provision a replacement.
</Tip>
